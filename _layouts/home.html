Hi, I'm Kyuha!

I’m an incoming PhD student in Informatics at University of California, Irvine (advisor: Professor Yunan Chen). My research areas are Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Health Informatics where my research aims to increase accessibility and sustainability of mental healthcare.

I am interested in how technology can facilitate tracking and sensemaking of people’s lived experiences with mental health issues. I pay close attention to people who already use technology for their mental health needs and how we can improve the status quo on behalf of them. I take qualitative human-centered approaches to explore these topics.

Prior to my PhD, I received a M.A. in Communication at Seoul National University (advisors: Professor Joonhwan Lee and Hajin Lim at the HCI+D Lab) and a B.S. in Information and Interaction Design at Yonsei University (advisor: Professor Younah Kang).
